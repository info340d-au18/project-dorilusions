Project Proposal

Topic Overview

Taiko Kai is the University of Washington's first Taiko ensemble, and was founded in 2013 by three sisters at UW. Taiko Kai's goal is to promote and spread appreciation Japanese culture through the art of traditional Japanese drumming by evoking a sense of heritage and social understanding. Since Taiko's founding, Since then, Taiko Kai has performed at various campus events such as FIUTS's CulturalFest, New Student Orientation, and the TSA Night Market. They have also performed at noteworthy events in the Seattle area such as Redmond City Lights and Sakura-Con.

However, the club currently has no web functional web platform. Currently, information about Taiko Kai is scattered across multiple Facebook and campus centers. With a large portion of Taiko Kai's performance and equipment funding coming from performance bookings/donations, it would be beneficial for Taiko Kai to have a hub for booking and information about the club. Additionally, Taiko Kai struggles to recruit new members each year, with most new members joining the club through on-campus promotions (RSO Fair) and word of mouth. As such, having a web application for potential members to express interest and discover the club would also be helpful. Finally, all member-related documents and information are stored in Google Drive. As such, we would like to create a webpage that would consolidate all current information. The website would not only be a source for anyone intereted in discovering more about Taiko, but would also be a way for potential members and bookers to contact Taiko Kai directly. In addition, the website would be a resource for current Taiko members as a central point of access for meeting notes, sheet music, and practice videos are currently stored in several Google Drives.

Potential Applications

Some of our web's potntial applications are:

  • 1. Performances Related Acvitities

      This includes functions such as booking performances, looking up upcoming performances, and getting in touch with the club UW Taiko Kai performs for the students. Thus we want to make sure that they have easy access towards those information. This application of our website allows user to find the information about our performances. This also allows organizations to book appointment with us more easily when they have the need.

  • 2. Account Management

      We want to have a personal portal for our users. The portal helps the user to organize their information. For the general public, they can view the performances they scheduled, they booked, or they plan to attend. For members, they can access club related files such as Meeting Notes, attendance, documents on who's performances, Taiko Kai Constitution. Club directors have more functions as an Admin account. They can manage members, control files, and make changes to certain things on the website. All users have access towards performance videos and some of the basic Taiko techniques.

  • 3. Taiko Related Info

      UW Taiko Kai also aims to educate the general public about the culture of Taiko. Thus, we dedicate this section to the history of Taiko and UW Taiko Kai. The website will contain the history Taiko as a musical instrument in Japan and how it spreads around the world. It will also talk about the history of UW Taiko Kai, as a RSO, its developments, and its current members.
